Our Monthly Book Club

About the club

Our book club is a space for anyone who is passionate about reading to come together and share their thoughts about our pick of the month.

We meet on the second Saturday of every month at 2pm at The Book Cafe, a wonderful little place with a cozy atmosphere for readers.

We provide a small snack, but coffee and tea are not included. The beverages at The Book Cafe are superb and since we use their space free of charge, we encourage you to have a drink here to support this collaboration.

While we mainly discuss our book of the month, this is also a social gathering where you're encourgaed to discuss other favourite reads and make friends in the book lovers community.

Finally, it's important to note that we are an inclusive community. Everyone is welcome here. Please be respectful at all times to keep this space fun and safe for all.

This month's read

We will be reading The Kingdoms by Natasha Pulley

Synopsis:
A time twisting alternative history that asks whether it's worth changing the past to save the future, even if it costs you everyone you've ever loved. (Goodreads)

We're very excited about this beautifully-written book set in Scotland, and can't wait to discuss it with you.

Just to get you really keen for this fantastic book, here are a few more words from some experts...

"The Kingdoms is an intricate and complex but ultimately very satisfying read, blending time slip and historical fiction with the pace of a thriller and the sweetness of a love story. A unique book."
Sinead Crowley
"Natasha Pulley has created a world so richly immersive that I felt real shock returning to reality. At once fiercely original, joyous and horrifying, The Kingdoms is an awe-inspiring feat of imagination and passion which had me in tears by the end."
Catriona Ward
"All time-travel plots are fraught with paradox, but not all rise to Pulley's level of tricky cleverness, and few of those trickily clever books rise to her level of emotional intensity. Suspenseful, philosophical, and inventive, this sparkling novel explores the power of memory and love."
Kirkus Reviews
